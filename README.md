# Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn behaviour using Power BI to identify the factors contributing to customer attrition and revenue loss.

Customer churn is one of the most important business metrics for subscription-based and service-oriented companies. Understanding why customers leave allows organizations to develop targeted retention strategies, improve customer satisfaction, and reduce revenue loss.

The dashboard provides an interactive view of customer demographics, contract types, tenure, internet services, and other key factors influencing churn.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Customer retention is often more cost-effective than customer acquisition. The objective of this analysis is to answer critical business questions such as:

- What percentage of customers are churning?
- How much revenue is lost due to churn?
- Which customer groups are most likely to leave?
- Does contract type influence churn behaviour?
- How does customer tenure affect retention?
- Which internet service customers are most at risk of churning?

## 📈 Key Visualizations

### Churn per Contract Type

Compares churn across different contract categories.

**Insight**

- Customers on month-to-month contracts have significantly higher churn than customers on one-year or two-year contracts.

---

### Churn per Tenure

Shows churn distribution across customer tenure groups.

**Insight**

- Customers within their first year are the most likely to churn.
- Churn decreases as customer tenure increases.

---

### Churn per Internet Service

Highlights churn across internet service types.

**Insight**

- Fiber Optic customers account for the highest number of churned customers.
- DSL customers churn less frequently.

---

### Churn by Partner Status

Examines churn behaviour based on whether customers have a partner.

**Insight**

- Customers without partners represent the majority of churned customers.

---

## 🔍 Key Business Insights

### 1. Contract Length Drives Retention

Month-to-month customers exhibit the highest churn levels, suggesting that longer-term contracts improve customer loyalty.

### 2. Early Customer Lifecycle Is Critical

The highest churn occurs within the first year of service, highlighting the importance of onboarding and early engagement programs.

### 3. Fiber Optic Customers Require Investigation

Despite being a premium service, Fiber Optic customers show the highest churn levels, indicating potential issues related to pricing, service quality, or competition.

### 4. Significant Revenue Impact

Customer churn has resulted in over **£139,000** in lost revenue, demonstrating the financial importance of retention initiatives.
